    Experiences & expertises

    Jan is an accomplished IT professional with over 30 years of experience, specializing in cloud solutions, Agile methodologies, and project management. His expertise spans technical leadership, engineering, architecture, and product ownership. Since 2011, he has been at the forefront of the cloud industry, driving innovation and developing premium cloud solutions for multinational companies like DXC Technology and Hewlett-Packard.

    As a certified Product Owner, he has successfully led cross-functional teams with engineers from all around the globe in delivering high-value projects using Agile and SAFe methodologies. Quite often, he thinks broadly and improves products with visionary inputs, ensuring long-term value for stakeholders. His work includes defining product visions, managing backlogs, and overseeing complex infrastructure developments, such as DXC's Managed Multicloud Solution. Beyond technical expertise, he excels in fostering collaboration across global teams, ensuring alignment with strategic goals, and maintaining a user-focused approach.

    A Swiss citizen fluent in both French and English, he combines adaptability, creativity, and a visionary mindset, making him a highly effective leader in managing large-scale projects with global, national, and even regional footprints. Project size does not matter as long as he finds a challenge or a highly valuable aspect (to his eyes) within it.

    Leadership

    As a Product Owner, I lead a team of 9 engineers and developers daily, spread across Europe and Asia. On some projects, I have also supervised up to 30 engineers, including talents from North and South America, for over 7 years. My expertise lies in managing and delivering complex and large-scale projects, always within the given deadlines. Working with multicultural and dispersed teams around the world adds an extra challenge that I successfully tackle.

    As a leader, I lead by example and empower teams and individuals. My expertise lies in work definition, organization, prioritization, and follow-up. I value communication and collaboration, but I also make timely decisions. Conflicts1 are rare and minor because important decisions are discussed between all parties, always prioritizing2 company interests over individual interests.

    I leverage my experience as a customer and service provider to anticipate customer’s expectations. By actively listening to all stakeholders, I optimize development processes, avoiding unnecessary rework and enabling seamless integration of future enhancements. This approach results in cost savings, improved stability, and faster time-to-market. I also consider external and internal needs, balancing product value with technical debt.

    While my direct involvement in development has decreased, I understand technical aspects strongly. I provide advice (sometimes after thorough explanations) to address challenges. My focus lies in the global solution, ensuring consistency, preventing duplicate work we can see in large teams, and emphasizing development, optimization, security, and especially automation.

Personally, I’ve encountered conflict only a few times—two or three at most—but through countless discussions and observations, one key insight stands out: a pervasive lack of communication. This issue isn’t just confined to leaders; it exists at all levels within companies. When communication breaks down, objectives become unclear, priorities are misaligned, and collaboration suffers. At its core, conflict often arises because people aren’t on the same page. Yet, the shared goal should always be simple: doing what’s best for the organization as a whole.
